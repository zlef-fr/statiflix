Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

Statiflix is built so that your viewing history stays yours. This page explains exactly what happens to your data — there isn't much to explain, by design.

What the extension does

When you click “Analyze my history”, the extension reads your own Netflix viewing-activity using the session already in your browser, and computes all statistics locally on your device. Your history is stored only in your browser's local extension storage. It is never uploaded anywhere.

What we never collect

  • No account, email, or login.
  • No analytics, no tracking pixels, no advertising IDs.
  • No IP logging tied to your data.
  • No viewing history sent to any server in readable form — ever.

Optional: mobile sync

If you choose to open your stats on your phone, the extension encrypts a snapshot with a key generated on your device (AES-256-GCM). Only the resulting ciphertext is uploaded to our relay, under a random ID, and auto-deletes after 24 hours. The decryption key travels only inside the QR code / URL fragment — it is never sent to the server, so the relay cannot read what it stores.

Show artwork & genres

Banner art and genres are read from Netflix's own API, in your own session, at the same time as your history — nothing extra is sent anywhere. As an optional fallback, if a title has no Netflix artwork the extension can look it up by title name only (no identity, no watch data) via our TMDB proxy; this is off unless that service is available. Titles with no artwork simply show a coloured placeholder.

Open source

Every line is auditable on GitHub. Statiflix is not affiliated with, or endorsed by, Netflix.
